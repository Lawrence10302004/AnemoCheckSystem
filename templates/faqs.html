Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about AnemoCheck and anemia detection.

General Questions

AnemoCheck is a web-based application that uses machine learning to detect anemia from Complete Blood Count (CBC) test results. It analyzes key parameters like hemoglobin, red blood cell count, hematocrit, and mean corpuscular volume to determine if you might have anemia and suggests possible anemia types.

No, AnemoCheck is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. It is designed as an educational and informational tool to help you understand your CBC results. Always consult with a healthcare professional for medical advice and proper diagnosis.

AnemoCheck has been trained on a large dataset of CBC results and has a high accuracy rate. However, like any machine learning model, it's not perfect and should be used as a tool to complement, not replace, professional medical evaluation. The system provides a probability with each prediction to indicate its confidence level.

Technical Questions

AnemoCheck requires four key values from your CBC test:

  • Hemoglobin (HGB) - measured in g/dL
  • Red Blood Cell Count (RBC) - measured in million cells/mcL
  • Hematocrit (HCT) - measured in percentage (%)
  • Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV) - measured in fL

These values are standard in most CBC tests. You can find them in your lab results.

AnemoCheck uses a machine learning algorithm that analyzes patterns in your CBC values. Rather than simply checking if values fall outside normal ranges (which traditional methods do), our model looks at the relationship between all four parameters and recognizes patterns associated with anemia that might not be obvious by looking at individual values alone.

Medical Questions

Anemia is a condition in which you don't have enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues. Having anemia can make you feel tired and weak. There are many forms of anemia, each with its own cause. Anemia can be temporary or long term, and it can range from mild to severe.

Common symptoms of anemia include:

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Pale or yellowish skin
  • Irregular heartbeats
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Chest pain
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Headaches

However, some people with mild anemia may not have any symptoms. Always consult a healthcare professional if you suspect you have anemia.

If AnemoCheck suggests you have anemia, you should:

  1. Consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis
  2. Share your CBC results and the AnemoCheck analysis with your doctor
  3. Discuss appropriate next steps, which might include additional testing
  4. Follow your doctor's recommendations for treatment if anemia is confirmed

Remember that AnemoCheck is a screening tool and not a diagnostic device. Only a healthcare provider can officially diagnose anemia and recommend appropriate treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AnemoCheck?

What is anemia?

Anemia is a condition marked by a reduced number of red blood cells or abnormalities in complete blood count (CBC) results, which can affect the blood's ability to transport oxygen throughout the body. This may lead to symptoms such as fatigue, weakness, pale skin, and shortness of breath.

How is anemia classified?

Anemia is classified using trained machine learning algorithms that analyze patterns within complete blood count (CBC) results. These algorithms are designed to identify key indicators and variations associated with different types and severities of anemia. By learning from a large dataset of labeled examples, the model can accurately categorize new cases, enabling faster and more consistent classification compared to manual evaluation.

What causes anemia?
  • Iron deficiency
  • Vitamin deficiencies (B12, folate)
  • Chronic diseases
  • Blood loss
  • Inherited conditions (e.g., sickle cell anemia, thalassemia)
  • Bone marrow problems

How do I get my CBC results?

Complete Blood Count (CBC) results are typically obtained through a blood test ordered by a healthcare provider. Once you receive your CBC test results, you can input the relevant values into AnemoCheck for anemia classification.

Can I use AnemoCheck without creating an account?

No, AnemoCheck requires account creation to use its features. This ensures that your data history is securely stored and accessible only to you, allowing you to track changes over time.

How often should I check my CBC results?

The frequency of checking your CBC results depends on your individual health needs. For generally healthy individuals, an annual test may be sufficient. However, if you have been diagnosed with anemia or are at risk, your healthcare provider may recommend more frequent monitoring.

Are the recommendations medically reviewed?

AnemoCheck provides general recommendations based on WHO guidelines. These are intended as informational guidance and not as a substitute for personalized medical advice. Always consult with a healthcare provider for specific treatment recommendations.

What technology powers AnemoCheck?
  • Python with Flask for the web application

How is the machine learning model trained?

The model is trained using synthetic data that includes relevant complete blood count (CBC) values. Various machine learning algorithms were applied to ensure accurate and reliable anemia classification.

Is AnemoCheck available as a mobile app?

Currently, AnemoCheck is available as a web application that works on both desktop and mobile browsers. A dedicated mobile app is planned for future releases.

Can I export my data?

Yes, AnemoCheck allows you to export your historical data in CSV format from the History page. This feature helps you share your data with healthcare providers if needed.
